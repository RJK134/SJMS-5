1Strategic Planning & Initiation ▼
🔗 Institutional Strategy Alignment
Review institutional learning and teaching strategy, strategic plan, and faculty priorities. Identify strategic fit with organisational goals.
👥 Programme Team Formed
Establish core team: Programme Lead, Academic Coordinators, Module Leaders, External Advisor. Define roles and responsibilities.
📋 Initial Proposal Drafted
Develop outline programme proposal: title, level (FHEQ), mode of delivery, student cohort, broad learning aims, estimated student numbers.
📈 Market Demand Assessment
Conduct market research: stakeholder consultation, UCAS trends analysis, graduate employment data, skills gap identification.
🚪 GATE: Strategic Approval
Academic Strategy Committee reviews proposal alignment with institutional direction and approves progression to Phase 2.
2Business Case Development ▼
🔍 Market Analysis
Competitor review: analyse existing programmes in UK and internationally. Demand forecasting: project recruitment targets. UCAS trends: historical application patterns for similar disciplines.
💰 Financial Modelling
Income projections based on student fees and other revenue. Cost analysis: staffing, facilities, marketing, systems. Break-even analysis and ROI calculations.
🏢 Resource Planning
Staffing requirements and recruitment timescale. Facilities and estate needs. IT infrastructure and learning platform requirements. Library and information resources.
📢 Recruitment Strategy
Marketing plan, recruitment channels, target markets, international recruitment considerations, employer partnerships for recruitment.
🚪 GATE: Business Viability
Finance & Planning Committee approves financial projections, confirms resource availability, and authorises progression to Phase 3.
3Academic Design ▼
📐 Programme Specification Development
Detailed programme specification: aims, teaching and learning strategy, entry requirements, progression rules, assessment methods, programme structure (modules/credits).
🎯 Programme Learning Outcomes (PLOs)
Define PLOs using Bloom's taxonomy (Knowledge, Skills, Competence). Ensure alignment with FHEQ level descriptors. Specify outcomes at exit points (diploma, degree).
📚 Module Design & Descriptors
Design each module: learning outcomes aligned to PLOs, content outline, assessment methods, credit weighting. Create comprehensive module descriptors for student handbook.
✍️ Assessment Strategy
Assessment methods aligned with QAA Quality Code. Mix of formative and summative assessment. Clear marking criteria and feedback mechanisms. Academic integrity and student support provisions.
🗺️ Curriculum Mapping
Matrix of modules × PLOs showing where each outcome is Introduced, Developed, or Assessed. Ensures comprehensive coverage and appropriate sequencing.
🔐 Entry Requirements & Progression
Clear entry criteria (qualifications, experience). Progression rules between levels. Exit point awards. Support for students with additional needs.
🏛️ PSRB Alignment
If applicable, ensure alignment with Professional, Statutory and Regulatory Bodies (e.g. BPS, RIBA). Accreditation pathways identified.
🚪 GATE: Academic Quality
Faculty Learning & Teaching Committee reviews programme specification, learning outcomes, and assessment strategy for academic rigor and alignment with QAA Quality Code.
🚪 GATE: Resource Adequacy
Faculty Board confirms sufficient staffing expertise, facilities, and resources to deliver programme to specified standard.
4External Review & Validation ▼
👨‍🎓 External Subject Expert Review
Recruit experienced external subject matter expert(s). Provide comprehensive documentation. Expert reviews programme specification, curriculum design, and learning outcomes for academic standards and disciplinary currency.
🤝 Industry/Employer Consultation
Engage employers and industry practitioners. Gather feedback on employability focus, skills development, graduate competencies. Incorporate into programme design.
👔 Validation Panel Assembled
Panel composition: Independent Chair, Internal Academic Member, External Subject Specialist, External Industry Representative, Student Representative, QA Officer, Secretary. All undergo briefing on institutional context and validation process.
📦 Pre-Validation Documentation
Comprehensive validation dossier: programme specification, module descriptors, curriculum mapping, assessment strategy, resource plans, external expert report, employer feedback, evidence of consultation.
🎤 Formal Validation Event
Validation panel meets with programme team. Presentation of programme, Q&A session, review of documentation. Panel deliberations and judgement on academic standards and viability.
📋 Conditions, Recommendations, Commendations
Panel issues formal validation report: Conditions (must be met before approval), Recommendations (should be considered for enhancement), Commendations (areas of excellence).
🚪 GATE: External Validation
Independent Validation Panel confirms academic standards, market viability, and readiness for delivery. Conditions must be satisfied before Phase 5 approval.
5Approval & Implementation ▼
✅ Conditions Met & Verified
Programme team addresses all validation conditions. QA Office verifies completion. Evidence submitted to Academic Board for final sign-off.
📊 Programme Created in SITS/SJMS
Administrator creates programme record in student record system. Establishes programme code, version control, approval date. Links to modules in system.
🎓 UCAS Listing Setup
Register programme with UCAS. Configure entry requirements, course details, tariff information. Set recruitment cycle and application deadlines.
📱 Marketing Materials Produced
Prospectus entries, web content, brochures, social media materials. Admissions team trained on programme specifics. UCAS Apply integration configured.
⚙️ n8n Workflow Automation
Trigger "Programme Approved" workflow: Creates student intake profile, configures assessment templates, initiates timetabling, registers programme with data warehouse.
👨‍💼 Staff Appointment & Briefing
Recruit and appoint module leaders and teaching staff. Conduct briefing sessions on programme aims, assessment strategy, student support mechanisms, learning analytics.
📖 Student Handbook Prepared
Comprehensive handbook with programme aims, module details, assessment criteria, progression rules, support services, academic integrity policies, appeals procedures.
🚪 GATE: Final Approval
Academic Board formally approves programme for delivery. Authorises UCAS registration and student recruitment to commence.
6Delivery & Monitoring ▼
🏫 Programme Runs
Students recruited and enrolled. Teaching delivery commences according to timetable. Learning resources and support services activated.
📝 Module Evaluations
Conduct module feedback surveys at end of each module/semester. Analyse feedback data. Module leaders respond to feedback with action plans.
🗣️ Student Feedback
Student-Staff Liaison Committee (SSLC) meetings. National Student Survey (NSS) results analysed. Programme-specific feedback mechanisms. Feedback loop closure demonstrated.
🔍 External Examiner Reports
Appointed external examiner reviews assessment and student work. Annual report submitted covering standards, assessment rigour, learning outcomes achievement. Recommendations addressed.
📊 Annual Programme Report (APR)
Comprehensive annual review: feedback analysis, performance data, student outcomes, teaching innovations, action plans. Submitted to Faculty and QA Office.
📈 KPI Tracking
Monitor: Student retention rates, achievement rates by module, degree classification distribution, student satisfaction scores, graduate employability, progression to further study.
🔄 Action Plans Implemented
Based on feedback, external examiner reports, and KPI data, implement enhancement actions. Track completion and effectiveness of actions.
7Review & Enhancement ▼
🔍 Annual Monitoring Review
Conducted each year. Review APR data, feedback trends, KPIs, and effectiveness of previous action plans. Identify emerging issues and enhancement opportunities.
🔄 Periodic Review (5-6 Year Cycle)
Comprehensive review of entire programme. Examine curriculum relevance, learning outcomes currency, assessment methods, student outcomes over time. Engage external advisor.
♻️ Revalidation
Formal revalidation event (similar to initial validation) to confirm programme continues to meet academic standards. May involve revised specification or modest enhancements.
✏️ Programme Modification
Major modifications: Significant curriculum changes, new learning outcomes, assessment method changes - require validation event. Minor modifications: Module content updates, staff changes - documented approval.
⏸️ Programme Suspension/Withdrawal
If programme no longer viable or strategically aligned, plan suspension or withdrawal. Manage student transition, graduation support, and teach-out arrangements.
🌟 Enhancement-Led Review
Proactive identification of excellence and innovation. Document best practice. Share learning across programmes. Drive continuous improvement culture.
🔁 Continuous Improvement Cycle
Insights from Phase 7 feed back to Phase 3 (Academic Design) for iterative curriculum enhancement. Create feedback loops between delivery, monitoring, and design.

🔧 Technical Architecture

n8n Workflow Automation Event-driven workflows manage state transitions. "Programme Approved" trigger initiates cascading tasks: SITS updates, UCAS registration, intake profile creation, assessment template deployment.
SJMS v4 Pipeline Tracking Programme proposals progress through defined status stages: Draft → Submitted → Validated → Approved → Active → Under Review → Archived. Status linked to approval gates with audit trail of all decisions.

📋 Quality Assurance

QAA Code OfS Conditions FHEQ Aligned

QAA UK Quality Code Alignment Programme design and monitoring aligned with QAA Quality Code expectations: clear learning outcomes, rigorous assessment, effective feedback, continuous improvement.
OfS Conditions of Registration Compliance with Office for Students requirements: academic standards, teaching quality, student support, financial viability tracking through monitoring framework.
FHEQ Level Framework All programmes designed to FHEQ level descriptors. Learning outcomes mapped to Framework for Higher Education Qualifications in England, Wales, Northern Ireland, Scotland.

🔗 Key Integrations

  • UCAS: Programme listings, application processing
  • SITS: Student enrolment, assessment recording
  • Learning Platform: Module delivery and resources
  • Analytics: KPI tracking and dashboards
  • Email: Notifications at approval gates

  • ☑ Validation event conducted
  • ☑ Conditions addressed
  • ☑ Final approval obtained
