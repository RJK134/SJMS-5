## Responsibilities

* Define "to-be" finance journeys for UG/PGT/PGR including registration-triggered fee creation and change-of-circumstance recalculation. (Fees-Funding-PID-V2.6-approved-06.10.16.pdf)
* Specify operational controls: approvals, audit trail, segregation of duties, and period close expectations (where applicable). (2019-11-08_UEL_SAM-Debt-Management-Review_Report_v0-2-with-RK-Highlights.docx)
* Prioritise integrations: ERP/GL replication, payment gateway, SLC, banking outputs, communications templates. (Fees-Funding-PID-V2.6-approved-06.10.16.pdf)
* Own the student finance product backlog, ensuring alignment between policy intent and system capability.
* Define role-based permissions matrix for finance operations (who can post, approve refunds, close periods, override sanctions).
* Ensure student-facing finance journeys (portal statements, payment screens, instalment plan views) meet accessibility and CMA requirements.

## Key Inputs

* Institutional fee/funding policies, payment acceptance strategy, refund policy. (Fees-Funding-PID-V2.6-approved-06.10.16.pdf)
* External constraints (SLC confirmation rules, funding rules). (heinfo.slc)
* Regulatory requirements (OfS, CMA, HMRC where applicable).
* Operational feedback from finance teams, student services, and registry.

## Key Outputs

* Product backlog with prioritised epics and user stories.
* Acceptance criteria for each finance capability area.
* Role-based permissions matrix.
* KPI/MI set (e.g., debt ageing, allocation errors, refunds turnaround, SLC confirmation timeliness). (Fees-Funding-PID-V2.6-approved-06.10.16.pdf)
* Finance process maps aligned to system design.

## Non-Goals

* Does not implement code; ensures build roles have testable specifications.
* Does not set institutional fee policy (that is a governance/committee decision).
* Does not perform day-to-day finance operations.
